# CTI — Cognitive Throughput Infrastructure

> *Toward an Operational Theory of Measurable Cognition*

**Version:** v2.0.0 &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Status:** Open Research &nbsp;|&nbsp; **License:** CC BY 4.0

---

## The Problem

Modern AI is built on a fundamental obsession: more parameters, more compute, more speed, more outputs.

Yet the industry still lacks something essential: **an operational theory of cognition.**

We measure tokens per second, latency, FLOPS, accuracy, benchmarks — but we don't formally measure the **flow of valid decisions**, cognitive efficiency, or the operational dynamics of intelligent systems.

**CTI is a response to that gap.**

├─────────────────────────────────────┤

The **Cognitive Throughput Layer** sits above models and agents. Its purpose: measure cognition, observe decision transformations, and optimize throughput under real constraints.

---

## First Law — The Cognitive Throughput Axiom

The operational capacity of an intelligent system can be measured by the rate at which it produces validated decisions over time.

$$I_t = \frac{\Delta D}{\Delta T}$$

| Variable | Definition |
|---|---|
| $I_t$ | Inference Throughput |
| $\Delta D$ | Validated Strategic Decisions |
| $\Delta T$ | Temporal Latency |

Intelligence under CTI is not evaluated solely by outputs — but by the **rate of validated cognition**.

---

## Second Law — The Cognitive Efficiency Principle

Optimal cognition is not that which produces the most outputs, but that which generates **high-quality decisions under limited constraints**.

$$E_c = \frac{Q}{C \cdot T}$$

| Variable | Definition |
|---|---|
| $E_c$ | Cognitive Efficiency |
| $Q$ | Decision Quality |
| $C$ | Computational Cost |
| $T$ | Temporal Latency |

Higher quality raises $E_c$. Higher cost or latency lowers it. The goal is not maximum output — it is **maximum cognitive utility under real constraints**.

## What CTI Is Not

CTI is **not** a foundation model, an agent, a prompt wrapper, a dashboard, or a traditional benchmark.

## What CTI Aims to Be

- A **cognitive observability infrastructure**
- An **operational theory** of cognitive throughput
- An **open standard** for measuring intelligent systems
- A **universal optimization layer** for decision systems
